Vocational Certification Programs

Structured, hands-on curricula designed to help students transition from basic machine handling to commercial fashion tailoring.

Basic / Foundational

Basic Tailoring Course

3 Months Program

Designed specifically for beginners who have never handled a needle or sewing machine. This course establishes foundational motor controls and construction layout principles.

Syllabus Breakdown:

  • Machine Mechanics: Sewing machine assembly, controls, tension calibration, oiling, and troubleshooting.
  • Stitch Mastery: Hand stitching patterns, running stitches, overcasting, machine straight cuts, curves, and corners.
  • Measurement Systems: Taking accurate human anatomical measurements and reading pattern grids.
  • Garment Drafts: Basic drafts, fabric-saving layouts, tracing, and cutting techniques.
  • Stitched Outlines: Designing and assembling blouses, standard kurtis, salwars, simple skirts, and alterations.

Advanced / Professional

Advanced Dress Designing

6 Months Program

Tailored for students who already possess basic sewing skills and want to master fashion illustration, complex neck patterns, boutique cutting styles, and finishing controls.

Syllabus Breakdown:

  • Neck & Sleeve Details: Pattern templates for Boat necks, Chinese collars, halter designs, puffs, and designer sleeves.
  • Specialty Blouses: princess-cut blouses, padded designs, high back-neck shapes, and designer linings.
  • Modern & Western Wear: Gowns, modern one-piece dresses, styled lehengas, layers, and draperies.
  • Embellishments & Finishes: Concealed zippers, piping, cord loops, fancy hooks, and fabric buttons.
  • Boutique Enterprise: Fabric selection, costing, customer interaction, boutique management, and local marketing rules.
